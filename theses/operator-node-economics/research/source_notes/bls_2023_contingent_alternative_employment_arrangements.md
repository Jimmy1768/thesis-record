## Core Claims

- BLS publishes 2023 CPS supplement tables for contingent and alternative
  employment arrangements.
- Table 6 reports employed workers with alternative and traditional work
  arrangements on the sole or main job by usual full- or part-time status,
  occupation, industry, class of worker, and multiple-jobholding status.
- Alternative arrangement categories include independent contractors, on-call
  workers, temporary help agency workers, and workers provided by contract
  firms.

## Evidence Used

The directly inspected BLS table reports July 2023 counts and shares for
alternative and traditional work arrangements. It includes occupation rows,
industry rows, and class-of-worker rows. The inspected table also states that
wage and salary workers exclude the self-employed, and that the self-employed
include incorporated and unincorporated self-employed workers.

## Relevance To TCE-P011

This source can help distinguish traditional employment from independent
contractor, temporary-help, contract-firm, and self-employment arrangements by
occupation or industry. It is useful for a substitution-side baseline, but only
when paired with a remote-work source and an AI-exposure source.

## Objections Or Limitations

- The inspected source does not identify AI use, AI labor services, Operator
  Nodes, vendor spend, or output.
- It is worker-level labor-force evidence, not firm procurement evidence.
- It does not by itself distinguish remote contractors from on-site
  contractors.
- It is a periodic supplement, not a high-frequency annual panel.
