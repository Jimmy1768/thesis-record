## Evidence Category

- Task productivity: yes.
- Transaction-cost evidence: no.
- Firm-boundary evidence: no.
- Adoption-by-size evidence: no.

## Core Claims

- ChatGPT improved speed and quality on bounded professional writing tasks.
- Effects are task-dependent and may not generalize to fact-sensitive,
  high-context, or open-ended work.

## Evidence Used

The verified DOI record reports a preregistered online experiment in which 453
college-educated professionals performed occupation-specific incentivized
writing tasks, with half randomly assigned access to ChatGPT. It reports that
average task time decreased by 40 percent and output quality rose by 18
percent. The MIT working-paper PDF supplies the directly inspected methods and
discussion detail currently available locally.

## Relevance To Operator Node Hypothesis

The source supports AI reducing some bounded writing and documentation task
costs. It does not prove reduced transaction costs, reduced need for firms, or
Operator Node survival.

## Objections Or Limitations

- Tasks were bounded and relatively generic.
- The result may not generalize to precise factual work, regulated work,
  relationship-heavy work, or multi-stakeholder production.
- It does not measure search, contracting, monitoring, dispute, enforcement,
  liability, procurement, firm size, outsourcing, nonemployer share, or
  management-layer outcomes.
