## Bibliographic Reference

Alexander Bick, Adam Blandin, and David J. Deming, "The Rapid Adoption of
Generative AI," NBER Working Paper No. 32966, issued September 2024, revised
February 2025. DOI: 10.3386/w32966.

## Source Type

NBER working paper based on nationally representative U.S. surveys.

## Evidence Category

- Task productivity: weak self-reported time-savings evidence.
- Transaction-cost evidence: no.
- Firm-boundary evidence: no.
- AI adoption evidence: yes, at worker/population level, not firm-boundary
  level.

## Core Claims

- The paper reports nationally representative U.S. survey evidence on
  generative-AI use at work and at home.
- The NBER abstract states that by late 2024 nearly 40 percent of U.S. adults
  age 18-64 used generative AI.
- It reports that 23 percent of employed respondents used generative AI for
  work at least once in the previous week and 9 percent used it every workday.
- It estimates that 1 to 5 percent of work hours were assisted by generative
  AI, with respondent-reported time savings equivalent to 1.4 percent of total
  work hours.

## Evidence Used

The NBER page verifies the paper's metadata, DOI, revision date, abstract, and
data appendix listing. The inspected abstract supports adoption-speed and
worker-use claims only.

## Relevance To Operator Node Hypothesis

This is useful AI-adoption context, but it does not measure nonemployer
businesses, one-human firm output, outsourcing, management layers,
transaction-cost reduction, or firm-boundary change. It should not be used to
support Operator Node claims except to show that worker-level adoption is not
the missing firm-boundary evidence.

## Objections Or Limitations

- NBER working paper, not peer-reviewed final article.
- The inspected source was the NBER page and abstract, not full-text
  extraction.
- Survey self-reports are not administrative business outcomes.
- The source does not distinguish employer firms, nonemployers, contractors,
  or one-human businesses.
