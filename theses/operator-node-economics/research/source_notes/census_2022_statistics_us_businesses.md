## Core Claims

- SUSB is an annual series for U.S. establishments with paid employees.
- SUSB reports data by establishment industry and enterprise size.
- The series includes firm and establishment counts, employment during the
  week of March 12, and annual payroll.

## Evidence Used

The Census program page identifies SUSB as an annual series for U.S.
establishments with paid employees by establishment industry and enterprise
size, including number of firms and establishments, employment, and annual
payroll.

## Relevance To Operator Node Hypothesis

SUSB can provide employer-firm size baselines to compare with nonemployer data
or AI adoption sources. It can help track whether small employer firms, large
employer firms, or nonemployer businesses are gaining share, but it does not
itself identify AI adoption or one-human firms.

## Objections Or Limitations

- SUSB excludes nonemployer businesses.
- It does not measure AI adoption, transaction costs, management layers,
  contractors, vendor substitution, or firm-boundary transitions.
- Enterprise size and establishment employment are not equivalent to
  organizational hierarchy depth.
