## Core Claims

- AI improved performance for consultants on tasks within the AI frontier.
- AI worsened performance on some outside-frontier tasks.
- Users can misjudge when AI is reliable, creating verification and judgment
  problems.

## Evidence Used

The directly inspected Organization Science article reports a preregistered
experiment with 758 BCG knowledge workers assigned to no AI, GPT-4 access, or
GPT-4 plus prompt-overview conditions. On 18 within-frontier consulting tasks,
AI users completed 12.2 percent more tasks, worked 25.1 percent faster, and
produced higher-quality outputs. On a selected outside-frontier managerial
task, AI users were 19 percent less likely to produce correct solutions.

## Relevance To Operator Node Hypothesis

This is important counterevidence against broad one-human capability claims. It
supports adding verification cost, task selection, and frontier awareness to
the Operator Node cost model. It does not support a firm-boundary claim.

## Objections Or Limitations

- The setting is consulting work, not direct evidence about firm boundaries.
- The treatment was embedded in a consulting-work experimental setting, not a
  market/protocol contracting setting.
- The source measures task completion, time, quality, and correctness, not
  search, contracting, monitoring, dispute, enforcement, procurement, or
  organizational-boundary outcomes.
