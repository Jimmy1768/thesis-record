## Core Claims

- BFS provides timely, high-frequency information on new business applications
  and formations in the United States.
- Business Formation is defined as the formation of an employer, wage-paying
  business from a business application.
- BF4Q and BF8Q measure employer businesses originating from applications
  within four or eight quarters.
- BFS is based on the Longitudinal Business Database, Business Register, and
  EIN-application data.
- The current release reports May 2026 business applications and projected
  employer-business formations.

## Evidence Used

The main page states that BFS provides timely and high-frequency information
on new business applications and formations. The definitions page states that
business formation means formation of an employer, wage-paying business from a
business application, and defines high-propensity applications based on
features associated with payroll formation. The June 10, 2026 release reports
business applications and projected business formations within four quarters.

## Relevance To Operator Node Hypothesis

BFS can help test whether business applications and payroll-employer
formations change in AI-exposed sectors. It is also relevant to the
node-becomes-firm criticism because it measures transitions from application
to employer formation, not durable one-human operation.

## Objections Or Limitations

- BFS does not identify AI use.
- BFS formation is an employer/payroll concept, not nonemployer formation.
- BFS does not directly measure nonemployer survival, receipts, hidden
  contractors, or one-human Operator Node status.
- Business applications can include entities that never become operating firms.
