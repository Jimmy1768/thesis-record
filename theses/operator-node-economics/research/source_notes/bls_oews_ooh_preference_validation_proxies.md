## Core Claims

- OEWS produces annual employment and wage estimates for approximately 830
  occupations, with estimates for the nation, states, metropolitan and
  nonmetropolitan areas, and national occupational estimates for specific
  industries.
- BLS describes market research analysts as studying consumer preferences,
  business conditions, and other factors to assess product or service sales.
- BLS describes market research analysts as collecting data through methods
  including focus groups, interviews, market analysis surveys, public opinion
  polls, and questionnaires.
- BLS describes survey researchers as designing and conducting surveys and
  analyzing data, including surveys to understand opinions, preferences,
  beliefs, or desires.

## Relevance To TCE-P012

These sources can support a conservative public proxy baseline for parts of
`TCE-P012`:

- occupation employment and wage trends for market research analysts;
- occupation employment and wage trends for survey researchers;
- industry/occupation employment distribution where OEWS detail permits;
- a weak proxy for formalized preference-research labor.

They do not directly measure experiencers as a new class. They identify
existing adjacent occupations that can anchor a monitoring design.

## Objections Or Limitations

- BLS occupations are existing labor categories, not new economic classes.
- Market research and survey work are not identical to paid experiential
  validation, product testing, UX participation, taste panels, or AI-output
  evaluation.
- OEWS is employer-establishment based and does not directly measure
  nonemployer creators, platform evaluators, unpaid reviews, passive telemetry,
  synthetic users, or node-based work.
- Employment growth in these occupations would not prove Operator Node
  diffusion unless linked to AI-enabled production abundance and firm-boundary
  change.
