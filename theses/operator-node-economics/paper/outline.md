## Working Research Question

Under what conditions, if any, could AI-enabled Operator Nodes reduce the
relative cost of market or protocol coordination enough to substitute for some
functions historically organized inside firms or corporations?

This question does not assume that Operator Nodes are emerging, superior, or
durable. It frames the paper as a falsifiable investigation of comparative
governance costs.

## Proposed Section Architecture

### 1. Research Problem, Scope, and Non-Claims

Status: architecture only; not ready for prose.

Purpose:

- State the puzzle as a comparative-governance problem, not a claim that the
  corporation is obsolete.
- Frame the paper as V1 of a versioned forecast research program, not as a
  completed empirical proof.
- Define the object of inquiry as possible shifts in coordination, transaction,
  production, and organizational costs after AI adoption.
- Establish that task productivity evidence is insufficient unless it connects
  to firm-boundary or governance outcomes.
- State exclusions: no manifesto framing, no spiritual or philosophical
  foundation, no conclusion that the hypothesis is true.

Required components:

- Research question.
- Versioning rule: V1 forecast baseline; V2/V3 evidence checks; publish
  supportive, null, adverse, or contradictory updates.
- Scope limits by sector, time horizon, and type of work.
- Distinction between production-cost, transaction-cost, and governance-cost
  claims.
- Statement that the paper tests a hypothesis against objections and failure
  conditions.
- Separation between the broad thesis, the user's company as one
  implementation case, and any external successful case.

Do not draft yet:

- Any claim that Operator Nodes are replacing firms.
- Any claim that corporations are declining because of AI.
- Any normative claim that one organizational form should prevail.
