# Operator Node Definition

## Working Definition

An **Operator Node** consists of:

- one human operator;
- AI reasoning systems;
- AI execution systems;
- institutional memory;
- shared protocols;
- audit trails;
- automation infrastructure.

The node is capable of producing economic output that historically required
multiple employees, departments, or management layers.

This is a functional production and coordination architecture, not a legal
form. An Operator Node may legally operate as a sole proprietorship, LLC,
corporation, partnership, contractor account, or other wrapper. The legal
wrapper does not define the node.

## Boundary

An Operator Node is not:

- a freelancer;
- a gig worker;
- a traditional small business;
- a solo creator with generic tools;
- a corporation with fewer employees.

The proposed distinction is that the node combines human judgment, AI systems,
memory, protocol discipline, and automation infrastructure into a repeatable
economic production unit.

## Boundary Distinctions

| Category | Distinction |
| --- | --- |
| Freelancer | A freelancer sells labor or expertise through market contracts. A freelancer may use AI, but the term does not imply persistent AI memory, audit trails, standardized protocols, or automation infrastructure capable of substituting for organizational layers. |
| Gig worker | A gig worker is often coordinated by a platform for discrete tasks. An Operator Node is intended to own or control a broader production stack and sell higher-level outcomes, not just platform-mediated labor. |
| Small business | A small business is a size or policy category, often based on employees or receipts. An Operator Node may be a small business, but small business status does not imply one-human AI-mediated production. |
| Firm | In Coasean terms, a firm is a system of relationships where resource direction depends on an entrepreneur. An Operator Node may itself be a one-person firm if it organizes transactions through the operator's direction. |
| Corporation | A corporation is a legal entity with legal personality, continuity, limited liability, and capital/governance machinery. An Operator Node can use a corporate wrapper, but the hypothesis concerns productive architecture, not corporate personhood. |
| Network organization | A network organization is a multi-actor exchange form. An Operator Node may be an atomic participant in a network; a network of nodes is not itself one Operator Node. |

## Warning

Do not define Operator Node as "non-firm" or "non-corporation." The stronger
claim is not legal form. The claim is that one human plus AI, memory,
protocols, audit trails, and automation may perform production and coordination
functions that historically required multi-person firms or management layers.

## Open Definition Problems

V1 starting thresholds for classification are recorded in the canonical policy
file:

- `living_dissertation_app/config/living_dissertation_policy.yml`

These defaults are measurement rules, not evidence that the thesis is true.
Empirical validation and revision remain open.

- What minimum capabilities qualify a node?
- How much autonomy must the AI systems provide?
- Is the node defined by ownership, workflow, output, or coordination protocol?
- Can a small team be one node, or must the definition remain one human?
- How should failed or partial nodes be classified?
- What thresholds show that a successful node has become a firm in practice:
  payroll, employees, managerial layers, dedicated assets, or internal
  authority relations?
- How should hidden labor, subcontractors, offshore vendors, family labor, and
  white-label agencies be detected?

No final definition is accepted yet.
