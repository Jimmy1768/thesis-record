# Terminology

## Operator Node

Working term for the proposed AI-augmented economic production unit.

## Corporation

Use in the economic and organizational sense, not as a moral category. Research
must distinguish legal corporation, firm, hierarchy, employer, and productive
organization when precision matters.

## Transaction Costs

Costs of discovering, negotiating, coordinating, monitoring, enforcing, and
adapting economic exchange. Coase and Williamson are required research anchors.

## Coordination Costs

Broader working term for costs of aligning people, tasks, information,
decisions, incentives, documentation, and memory.

## Protocol-Based Coordination

Coordination through shared standards, interfaces, procedures, audit trails,
software, and explicit contracts rather than managerial hierarchy.

## Institutional Memory

The stored knowledge, context, history, procedures, decisions, and norms that
allow an organization to continue functioning across time.

## Economic Class Implications

Working label for the conditional class-structure forecast that may follow if
the Operator Node thesis proves directionally correct.

## Forecast Taxonomy

A forward-looking classification scheme used to make predictions testable. A
forecast taxonomy is not evidence and should not be treated as a conclusion.

## Creators

People or entities that initiate, own, design, orchestrate, or compound
productive systems. In this forecast, creators answer what should be made or
coordinated.

## Niche Human Providers

Humans paid because humanness, rarity, performance, care, embodied presence,
trust, ritual, or prestige is part of the product. This category includes
human work that may survive because the human presence itself is valued.

## Experiencers

Humans paid to evaluate, validate, compare, review, certify, feel, test, or
communicate whether outputs are desirable to humans. In this forecast,
experiencers answer whether AI-produced or node-produced output is good for
humans, where "good" may mean preferred, trusted, comfortable, meaningful,
beautiful, safe, prestigious, or worth choosing.

## Avoided Terms

Avoid spiritual or philosophical framing in this repo's argument foundation.
Terms from philosophical or religious traditions should not appear as
foundations for the paper.
